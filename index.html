History of the Water Wheel and Steam Engine

The water wheel was first invented by Richard Arkwright. He had invented the Water Wheel so that he could power his factories all day long. If his factory is powered all day long, workers can do more work and therefore get more profit for Richard Arkwright.


The steam Engine was then created. They had been used to pump water out of the deep underground mines. They were slow and expensive. Later on a Scottish inventor called James Watt met with a business man named Matthew Boulton at a scinece club known as the Lunar Society.Together they both developed a new type of steam engine which Watt had been working on. It had a new gear system that turned just as a river would.That is when factory owners then reaized they can power their factories by steam.

Water Power

Water power was used as it was the only source of power at that time. Water power was also a very important change as now people could work any time at the day, though they have to come whenever the owner or their manager tells them to come.
A few advantages of using Water Power was:

-It could be used day and night
-The water to power it was free



There were also a few disadvantages to the water wheel, they were:

-The water wheel could not operate when the water was low
-The water wheel could not operate when the water was frozen

So later there was another great invention.

Steam Power

Steam power was invented after people realized that the water power was just not reliable enough.
A few advantages of using Steam Power was:

-It could be used day and night, all 365 days
-There was a lot of coal in Britain



There were also a few disadvantages to the steam power, they were:

-Britain became polluted
-It would break down very often
